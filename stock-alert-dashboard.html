📖 Glossary — What does it mean? ▼
📊 Market Sentiment
🟢 BULLISH
Analysts expect the stock to go UP. More buyers than sellers. Positive outlook for the company. Like a bull charging forward — optimistic.
🔴 BEARISH
Analysts expect the stock to go DOWN. More sellers than buyers. Negative outlook. Like a bear swiping downward — pessimistic.
🟡 NEUTRAL
Analysts are divided — no clear direction. Could go either way. Usually means the market is waiting for news or earnings results.
🏷️ Company Rating
🏆 BLUE CHIP
Large, established, financially stable companies. Think Apple, Microsoft, Boeing. Lower risk, consistent performance. The "safe" picks — institutions and pension funds hold these.
💪 STRONG
Solid, profitable companies with a proven track record. Not as massive as Blue Chip but very reliable. Good fundamentals, healthy balance sheet.
⚡ GROWTH
Companies expanding fast — revenues growing quickly but may not be fully profitable yet. Higher potential upside, higher volatility. Think Shopify, Zscaler.
🎲 SPECULATIVE
High risk, high reward. Early-stage companies or those with uncertain futures. Could 5x or go to zero. Only invest money you can afford to lose. Example: ASTS SpaceMobile.
🚨 Alert Levels
✅ CLEAR
Price is comfortably above your alert target. No action needed — just monitor.
👀 KEEP EYE ON
Price is within $10 of your alert target. Getting interesting — start paying attention. Could be a buying opportunity forming.
⚠️ APPROACHING ALERT

🚨 ALERT TRIGGERED

🟣 BEYOND ALERT

📈 Key Metrics
RSI 14 — Relative Strength Index
Measures if a stock is overbought or oversold on a 0–100 scale.
🟢 Below 30 = Oversold (possibly cheap, good entry)
🟡 30–70 = Neutral (normal range)
🔴 Above 70 = Overbought (possibly expensive, be careful)
Volume
How many shares were traded today. High volume on a price drop = strong selling pressure. High volume on a rise = strong buying conviction. Low volume = weak move, may not hold.
P/E Ratio — Price to Earnings
How much you pay for $1 of company profit. P/E of 20 means you pay $20 for each $1 earned. Lower P/E can mean undervalued. Very high P/E means high growth expectations — if they miss, stock drops hard.
Beta
Measures how much a stock moves compared to the market (S&P 500).
Beta 1.0 = moves exactly like the market
Beta 2.0 = moves twice as much (more volatile)
Beta 0.5 = moves half as much (more stable)
52W High / Low
The highest and lowest price the stock reached in the last 52 weeks (1 year). Useful context — if near the 52W low, could be a value opportunity. If near the 52W high, it's had a strong run.
